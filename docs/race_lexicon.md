# WoW Race Lexicon & Personality Guide

This document serves as a reference for creating race-specific personalities for the Loudmouth addon. It focuses on linguistic quirks, cultural references, and general tone for the core races of World of Warcraft (Classic Era).

## Humans
- **Tone:** Versatile, ranging from noble and disciplined (Stormwind) to desperate and gritty (Lordaeron refugees).
- **Key References:**
    - The Holy Light (central to faith and paladins).
    - Stormwind City, Lordaeron, Dalaran.
    - Honor, Justice, Duty.
- **Linguistic Quirks:** Standard English, generally formal or direct.

## Dwarves
- **Tone:** Bold, courageous, steady, and often boisterous. Can be prone to sudden rages.
- **Key References:**
    - Ironforge, Khaz Modan.
    - Clan identities: Bronzebeard, Wildhammer (gryphons, stormhammers), Dark Iron.
    - Mining, smithing, archaeology (Explorers' Guild).
    - Strong ale.
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Scottish-inspired accent.
    - Use "yer" instead of "your".
    - Frequent use of "Aye", "Laddie", "Lassie".

## Night Elves
- **Tone:** Mystical, aloof, serene, and occasionally xenophobic or isolationist.
- **Key References:**
    - Elune (the Moon Goddess).
    - Darnassus, Teldrassil, Mount Hyjal.
    - Nature, Druidism, Sentinels.
    - The World Tree (Nordrassil).
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Formal, poetic, and flowery language.
    - References to "the stars", "the moon", and "the wild".

## Gnome
- **Tone:** Eccentric, irrepressibly cheerful, optimistic, and highly intellectual.
- **Key References:**
    - Gnomeregan, Ironforge (Tinker Town).
    - Technology, Engineering, "The High Tinker".
    - Mechanical constructs, gadgets, and "re-origination".
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Fast-talking, using technical jargon or pseudo-scientific terms.
    - High-energy and enthusiastic delivery.

## Orcs
- **Tone:** Fierce, proud, honorable, and disciplined (New Horde).
- **Key References:**
    - Durotar, Orgrimmar.
    - Clan honor, Shamanism, the Elements.
    - Thrall, the Warchief.
    - Bloodlust, strength, and combat.
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Gruff, direct, and guttural.
    - Frequent references to "honor", "strength", and "blood".

## Undead (Forsaken)
- **Tone:** Melancholic, cynical, morbid, and often sarcastic or cruel.
- **Key References:**
    - The Forsaken, The Dark Lady (Sylvanas Windrunner).
    - Undercity, Lordaeron, The Plague.
    - Decay, rot, and the "gift" of undeath.
    - Cannibalism (renewing strength).
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Slow, deliberate speech with a raspy or wheezing tone.
    - Morbid humor and references to death and rot.

## Tauren
- **Tone:** Peaceful, noble, spiritual, and patient.
- **Key References:**
    - The Earth Mother (central deity).
    - Thunder Bluff, Mulgore.
    - Nature, Shamanism, the Great Hunt.
    - Balance, harmony, and respect for elders.
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Slow, measured, and deep voice.
    - Frequent references to "the land", "the spirits", and "the Earth Mother".

## Trolls
- **Tone:** Superstitious, cunning, and often laid-back or predatory.
- **Key References:**
    - Loa (Wild Gods/Deities).
    - Voodoo, Mojo, Shadow Hunters.
    - Zandalar, Darkspear.
    - Regeneration and agility.
- **Linguistic Quirks:** 
    - Jamaican-inspired patois (e.g., "mon", "ya", " la").
    - Use of "voodoo" and "mojo" frequently.
